# Contract: Short-Heading Evidence

## Positive evidence

A short paragraph may have heading semantics only from explicit source structure or an already-supported universal source-form distinction.

## Forbidden evidence

Length, word count, capitalization, leading ordinals, punctuation, nearby prose length, language vocabularies, book strings and substring matches cannot independently promote or hint a heading.

## Precedence

Explicit heading semantics are preserved. Authoritative body, attribution, caption, list, table and image classifications are not overwritten. Existing form recovery remains bounded to source metadata.

## No-signal result

The paragraph remains as received. Missing structure is accepted; downstream assembly and validation do not guess or gate it.
